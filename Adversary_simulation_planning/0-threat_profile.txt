Threat Actor Profile: FIN7

Target Sectors:
- Financial Services (banks, credit unions, payment processors)
- Hospitality (hotel chains, restaurants, casinos)
- Retail (point-of-sale environments, e-commerce)

Motivation:
FIN7 is a financially motivated cybercriminal group primarily focused on stealing
payment card data and conducting large-scale fraud for direct monetary gain.

Key Techniques:
1. T1566.001: Spearphishing Attachment (Initial Access)
2. T1059.001: PowerShell (Execution)
3. T1055.001: Dynamic-link Library Injection (Defense Evasion / Privilege Escalation)

Associated Tools:
- CARBANAK (banking trojan used for fraudulent transfers)
- HALFBAKED (backdoor used for persistent access)

Known Campaign:
Carbanak Campaign (2014–2015): FIN7, in coordination with the Carbanak group,
targeted over 100 financial institutions across 30+ countries by spearphishing
bank employees and deploying the Carbanak malware to manipulate banking systems.
The campaign resulted in estimated losses exceeding $1 billion USD through
fraudulent SWIFT transfers, ATM cashouts, and account manipulation.
