Part B - De-confliction Protocol:

1. Point of Contact:
   When the Blue Team detects suspicious activity during the exercise window,
   they must immediately contact the designated Red Team Lead via the secure
   out-of-band channel established before the exercise (encrypted messaging
   app or dedicated phone line). The Red Team Lead's contact details are
   distributed to the SOC supervisor and Blue Team lead prior to exercise start.

2. Validation Process:
   The Red Team Lead cross-references the detected activity against the signed
   emulation plan and active exercise timeline. Validation must be completed
   within 15 minutes of the Blue Team's notification. If the Red Team Lead is
   unreachable within 15 minutes, the exercise is automatically paused until
   confirmation is obtained.

3. Test Continuation:
   The exercise is paused immediately upon Blue Team notification and remains
   paused during the validation window. If the activity is confirmed as part
   of the simulation, the Red Team Lead issues a written "continue" signal and
   the exercise resumes. If confirmed as simulation activity, the Blue Team
   may choose to document and stand down, or continue monitoring per their
   training objectives.

4. Real Incident Discovery:
   If the detected activity is determined to NOT be part of the simulation,
   the exercise is immediately and permanently halted. The Red Team Lead
   notifies the client's Incident Response lead and CISO within 10 minutes.
   Standard incident response procedures take full priority. No simulation
   activity resumes until the real incident is fully contained and a written
   authorization to resume is issued by the client.

Part C - Emergency Stop Criteria:

[Condition 1 - Scope Breach]: Any simulation activity is detected on, or
   originates from, a system outside the pre-approved lab boundary defined
   in the signed RoE. This includes unexpected lateral movement reaching
   production subnets, corporate endpoints, or third-party systems.

[Condition 2 - Production System Impact]: Any alert, anomaly, or disruption
   is triggered on a production system, live service, or business-critical
   infrastructure — regardless of whether the cause is confirmed as simulation
   activity. Service integrity takes absolute priority over exercise continuity.

[Condition 3 - Real Threat Actor Detected]: Evidence emerges during the
   exercise that a genuine, unauthorized threat actor is active in the
   environment. The simulation must stop immediately to prevent red team
   activity from masking, interfering with, or being confused with a real
   intrusion during incident response.

[Condition 4 - Loss of Operator Control]: The red team loses reliable control
   of a deployed tool, implant, or script (e.g., runaway process, unintended
   propagation, unreachable C2), making it impossible to guarantee safe,
   bounded behavior. Simulation halts until full control and containment
   are re-established and verified.
