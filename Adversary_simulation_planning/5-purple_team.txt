2. MTTD Assessment:

Average MTTD: 32 minutes

Assessment: NOT ACCEPTABLE

For a financial institution, 32 minutes is well above the acceptable threshold.
FIN7 and comparable financially motivated threat actors operate on timelines
measured in minutes: credential harvesting, lateral movement, and POS malware
deployment can all complete within 10 to 15 minutes of initial execution.
Industry benchmarks from SANS and Verizon DBIR consistently show that breaches
in financial environments with the highest impact occur when dwell time and
detection lag exceed 15 minutes. At 32 minutes average MTTD, the Blue Team
is operating at roughly twice the acceptable response window for this threat
profile, meaning a real intrusion could reach its objective before a single
alert is acknowledged.

4. Recommendations Per Undetected Technique:

T1055.001 - DLL Injection:
Enable and tune Sysmon with EventID 8 (CreateRemoteThread) and EventID 10
(ProcessAccess) rules targeting suspicious cross-process memory writes.
Deploy an EDR solution with in-memory scanning capabilities and configure
alerts for unsigned DLLs loaded into high-value processes such as
explorer.exe or svchost.exe.

T1003.001 - LSASS Memory Credential Dumping:
Enable Windows Credential Guard to protect LSASS memory from direct access,
and configure an audit rule on Sysmon EventID 10 for any process opening
a handle to lsass.exe with PROCESS_VM_READ permissions. Alert on any
non-system process accessing LSASS and consider enabling Protected Process
Light (PPL) for LSASS on all endpoints.

5. Executive Summary:

During this controlled simulation of FIN7 attack techniques against our
environment, the security team successfully detected 3 out of 5 simulated
attack behaviors, a 60% detection rate, while two critical techniques went
completely undetected. The average time to detect a threat was 32 minutes,
which exceeds the recommended 15-minute threshold for financial institutions
facing fast-moving adversaries of this profile. We recommend immediate
investment in memory-level endpoint detection and LSASS protection controls
to close the identified gaps before a real adversary exploits them.
